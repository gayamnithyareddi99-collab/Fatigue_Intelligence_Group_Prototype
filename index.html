About & Mission

Transforming Reactive Safety Into
Predictive Workforce Intelligence

Fatigue Intelligence Group was founded on a clear premise: the UK's safety and workforce management systems are reactive. Incidents are investigated after they occur. Fatigue is identified after the shift ends. The cost in human lives, regulatory penalties, and lost productivity is enormous and largely preventable.

Our platform transforms fatigue risk management from a paper-based, post-incident practice into a governed, real-time intelligence system. We don't just monitor shift hours we model the neurological and physiological signature of fatigue at an individual and team level, providing the early warning intelligence that allows managers to intervene before risk becomes reality.

Built by supply chain, logistics, and safety professionals, FIG encodes operational expertise into a SaaS platform built specifically for the UK's high-risk sectors: logistics, healthcare, manufacturing, construction, and transport.

Platform Questions
Fatigue risk management is the process of identifying, monitoring, and mitigating safety risks posed by worker fatigue in shift-based and high-demand roles. UK employers have a duty of care under the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 and Working Time Regulations 1998. Fatigue Intelligence Group operationalises that duty turning it from a paper policy into a live, evidence-based safety system.
Logistics & supply chain, healthcare (NHS Trusts, care homes, emergency services), manufacturing, construction, and security services. Phase 1 focuses on logistics and healthcare the two sectors with the highest documented fatigue-related incident rates in the UK. Phase 2 expands nationally to Manchester, Birmingham, Bristol, and beyond.
FIG integrates with Garmin Health, Fitbit for Enterprise, Whoop, and access control systems via open APIs. Data flows securely into the fatigue risk model under explicit worker consent frameworks. No biometric data is shared with employers without worker consent the platform outputs a risk score, not raw physiological data.
Basic WTR tools check whether shifts meet minimum legal rest requirements but they cannot predict fatigue risk, integrate biometric data, perform incident forensics, or benchmark against sector peers. FIG unifies risk prediction, real-time monitoring, compliance automation, and forensic investigation into a single closed-loop platform.
